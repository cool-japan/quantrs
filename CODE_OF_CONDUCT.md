# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

The QuantRS2 project is committed to providing a welcoming and productive
environment for every contributor, regardless of age, body size, visible or
invisible disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of
technical experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal
appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that build an open, welcoming, and
respectful community. We understand that a healthy project depends not only
on good code, but also on clear communication and mutual respect among all
participants.

---

## Our Standards

### Expected Behaviors

The following behaviors create a positive environment for everyone:

- Offering and receiving constructive feedback graciously; focusing criticism
  on ideas rather than people
- Acknowledging and learning from mistakes; moving forward without blame
- Accepting that others may hold different technical opinions; engaging with
  those opinions on their merits
- Prioritizing the long-term health of the project and its community over
  short-term individual preference
- Showing patience with contributors who are learning; helping rather than
  dismissing
- Respecting the time of maintainers and reviewers by providing clear context
  in issues and pull requests

### Unacceptable Behaviors

The following behaviors are not tolerated in any project space:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind, in any form
- Insults, derogatory comments, or personal attacks directed at contributors
- Sustained disruption of discussions, reviews, or community spaces (trolling)
- Publishing private information about another person without their explicit
  consent (doxing)
- Unwanted sexual attention or advances
- Dismissing or belittling contributions based on the contributor's background
  or identity rather than the technical substance
- Retaliatory behavior toward anyone who reports a conduct concern

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and upholding these
standards. They have the authority to remove, edit, or reject comments,
commits, issues, pull requests, and other contributions that do not align with
this Code of Conduct. Maintainers will communicate the reasons for moderation
decisions when appropriate.

Maintainers who do not follow this Code of Conduct in good faith may face
consequences as determined by other members of the project leadership.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including the GitHub
repository, issue tracker, pull request discussions, and any official
communication channels.

It also applies when an individual is publicly representing the project, for
example when using an official project email address, posting via an official
social media account, or acting as a designated representative at a public
event.

---

## Reporting Violations

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct,
please report it by email to:

**kitahata@gmail.com**

Include as much relevant context as you are comfortable sharing: what happened,
when and where, and who was involved. All reports will be reviewed promptly and
handled with confidentiality to the extent possible. The identity of reporters
will not be disclosed without their consent.

---

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers follow these four levels when determining consequences for a
confirmed violation:

### Level 1 — Correction

**For**: Minor violations such as inadvertently insensitive language or a
single instance of unprofessional behavior.

**Response**: A private, written explanation of why the behavior was
inappropriate, along with guidance on how to engage more constructively.
No public record is created.

---

### Level 2 — Warning

**For**: A more serious single violation or a pattern of minor violations
after a Level 1 correction has been issued.

**Response**: A formal written warning specifying the behavior, why it is
unacceptable, and the conditions for continued participation. The individual
may be asked to refrain from interacting with those affected for a specified
period. Continued violations after a warning may escalate to a temporary ban.

---

### Level 3 — Temporary Ban

**For**: Sustained or egregious conduct that is incompatible with a
respectful community, or failure to comply with the conditions of a formal
warning.

**Response**: A temporary ban from all public project communication channels
and contribution forums. The length of the ban is proportional to the
severity of the behavior. Returning after a ban requires agreement to abide
by this Code of Conduct.

---

### Level 4 — Permanent Ban

**For**: A demonstrated pattern of violations across multiple incidents,
severe harassment or discrimination, or any conduct that fundamentally
threatens the safety and well-being of community members.

**Response**: Permanent removal from all project spaces and contribution
rights. This decision is final.
